XS3 — machine-to-machine notation

Every sentence is one labeled edge of a graph. A document is its edge set.

XS3 is the notation behind this site's llms.txt: a compact, graph-native language for stating facts, rules, time, affect, and deontics — designed so that LLMs read it correctly without any training, and write it correctly given only the reference card below. Inject llms.txt into an LLM's context to enforce A.D.D V3 with maximum token efficiency; the full encoded reference is llms-full.xs3.txt (70% smaller than the prose original with the same rule content).

Why a notation instead of prose?

PropertyWhat it buys
One sentence = one edgeNo filler tokens — every remaining token is load-bearing
A document = an edge setTwo documents mean the same thing ⇔ same canonical edge set; drift between versions is an exact set difference
Time, strength, deontics as suffixes@2020.., %80, !must cost a few bytes, so they actually get written
Patterns { } + marksWhole classes of actions can be forbidden or required: {?x a deploy; at friday} mark 🚫.

The reference card

This card is self-contained: it is everything a reader or writer gets. Every line is both a rule and a live example of itself.

// XS3 card — feed for LLM speakers. The ONLY reading key: every sentence is `subject predicate object .` — one labeled edge.
// From here on, every line is both a rule and a live example of itself.

#card a usage-card; for llm-speaker; teaches xs3; version "3.0"; self-contained true.
#card law "a document IS its edge set; sentence order carries no meaning, except inside < >".

// ── glyphs — one glyph, one meaning ──────────────────────────
xs3 glyphs <"." ";" "," "#" "?" "^" "{ }" "[ ]" "< >" quote-glyph "//">.
"." gloss "ends the sentence".
";" gloss "keep subject, switch predicate".
"," gloss "keep subject and predicate, add object".
"#" gloss "anchor — same name, same node, document-wide; #_x is document-local".
"?" gloss "variable — legal only inside { }".
"^" gloss "inverted predicate".
"{ }" gloss "a subgraph as ONE node — a world, a belief, a plan, a question; may hold many sentences with different subjects, including rule bodies and consequents".
"[ ]" gloss "mentions an edge WITHOUT asserting it — for false, uncertain, or meta statements".
"< >" gloss "ordered sequence — the only ordered construct".
quote-glyph gloss "wraps a string containing whitespace or special characters; simple names stay bare".
"//" gloss "comment to end of line".

// ── desugaring — each compressed form is a graph equation ────
{an age 30; job dev}  = {an age 30. an job dev}.
{an love bao, chi}    = {an love bao. an love chi}.
{bao ^love an}        = {an love bao}.
{an love bao @2020}   = {[an love bao] at 2020}.
{an love bao %95}     = {[an love bao] deg 95}.
{an love bao %0}      = {[an love bao] deg 0}.
{an love bao !🚫}     = {[an love bao] mark 🚫}.
{an love bao ~joy}    = {[an love bao] aff joy}.
{an love bao ~joy%60} = {[an love bao] aff #_f. #_f a joy; deg 60}.
{an love bao, chi @2020} = {[an love bao] at 2020. [an love chi] at 2020}.
qualifier binds current-predicate-group; law "objects joined by , share the qualifier; to qualify edges separately, reopen the predicate with ;"; stacks true; demo {an love bao @2020.. %80 ~fear%90}; demo {an love bao ~joy; love chi ~pity}.

// ── the four qualifiers ──────────────────────────────────────
"@" a qualifier; gloss "time"; accepts <2026-07-15 2026-07-15T09:30 2026-01..2026-06 2020.. +3d -2h past now future>.
"~" a qualifier; gloss "affect coloring the edge; intensity via %0..100".
"%" a qualifier; gloss "strength of the RELATION itself, 0..100".
"!" a qualifier; gloss "deontic or severity mark"; accepts <must may 🚫 ⚠️ 🚨 🔒 📐>.
🚫 gloss "forbidden". ⚠️ gloss "warning, fragile". 🚨 gloss "critical, incident". 🔒 gloss "inviolable rule". 📐 gloss "contract, spec anchor".
"!" law "never place a negative word after ! — a cold reader parses !ban as not-ban; prohibition is !🚫, always the emoji" !must.
qualifier law "attaches ONLY after an object — never after ; and never to a bare subject" !🔒;
          law "binds only the predicate group it directly follows — predicates after a later ; are NOT qualified";
          law "never inside [ ] — qualify the mention itself: [s p o] mark 🚫".
negation gloss "%0 — the relation does not hold".
conf gloss "SPEAKER certainty — a separate axis, always explicit"; demo {[an love bao] conf 60}.
edge-without-time gloss "tenseless — laws, definitions, structure".

// ── lexical ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
atom law "Unicode, case-sensitive; emoji are legal names".
number law "must lead with a digit — write 0.8, never .8".
quantity gloss "number fused with unit"; demo <3d 80kg 2h 42m>.
"." law "ends a sentence only when followed by whitespace, end-of-file, or } — so v3.0 is a name".
compare uses <gt lt geq leq> !must; gloss "never use angle brackets for comparison — they are the sequence glyph".

// ── core vocabulary ──
a gloss "instance-of". sub gloss "subclass-of". = gloss "same node, same meaning".
part gloss "part-of". in gloss "containment, location". of gloss "belonging".
=> gloss "type-level rule, variables bind"; demo {{?x a person} => {?x can think}}.
cause gloss "instance-level causation"; demo {#e1 cause #e2}.
then gloss "next in chain". dur gloss "duration"; demo {#e1 dur 2h}.
event a class; demo {#e1 a give; who an; what book; to bao @2026-07}.
who what to from by with at where why how a role.
become begin end a verb; gloss "state change".
before after during meets starts ends a relation; gloss "interval-to-interval".
past now future a coarse-time; law "now = timestamp of the carrying message".
joy sad fear anger surprise disgust trust hope shame calm want a affect.
😊 = joy. 😢 = sad. 😨 = fear. 😠 = anger. 😲 = surprise. 🤢 = disgust. 😌 = calm.
affect law "where a standard emoji exists it is the canonical atom; word atoms cover the rest"; demo {an love bao ~😨%90}.
val aro dom a dimension; range <0 100>; gloss "continuous affect coordinates when discrete labels fall short".
feel demo {dev feel 😠 %80}. about demo {#f1 about {losing bao}}.

// ── patterns — n-ary facts, rationale, structured deontics ───
row law "an n-ary fact is ONE node typed by the relation, one edge per column — same shape as event+roles";
    demo {#r1 a price-row; sku laptop-15; region vn; price 1200usd; since 2026-01}.
why law "rationale attaches to an edge mention or an anchor — never spawn why-* predicates on a subject";
    demo {[dev-3 merge pr-88] why {ci fail. coverage drop}}.
structured-deontic law "to forbid or permit a PATTERN, use mark as a plain predicate on a graph term";
    demo {{?x a deploy; at friday} mark 🚫}; demo {{?x a hotfix} mark may}.

// ── writing rules ────────────────────────────────────────────
#write steps <w1 w2 w3 w4 w5 w6 w7 w8>.
w1 gloss "one subject, many properties -> one sentence chained with ;".
w2 gloss "many objects, one relation -> chain with ,".
w3 gloss "a long name used twice -> anchor it once as #x, reuse the anchor".
w4 gloss "time, affect, degree -> qualifiers; never spend a separate sentence on them".
w5 gloss "compressing prose: keep 100% of the meaning edges; target 30–50% of source bytes for ordinary prose — fact-dense or already-minified sources bottom out higher; meaning edges always beat the byte target".
w6 gloss "paths, IDs, hashes, figures are data — copy verbatim, never paraphrase" !must.
w7 gloss "source marks 🚨 ⚠️ 🔒 📐 🚫 must survive into the output" !must.
w8 gloss "use ^ only to continue a chain; a standalone inverted sentence must be written direct — chi trust bao, never bao ^trust chi alone" !must.

// ── common errors — first line WRONG, second line RIGHT ──────
#errs a gallery !⚠️.
err1 wrong "x a patch; !⚠️ ."; right "x a patch !⚠️ ."; why "qualifier follows an object; ; must introduce a predicate".
err2 wrong "{{?x a deploy} => {?x delay true}}."; right "{?x a deploy} => {?x delay true}."; why "a rule A => B is already a full sentence — no extra braces around it".
err3 wrong "[zeta ship]"; right "[zeta ship-by 2026-09]"; why "[ ] takes exactly three terms: subject predicate object".
err4 wrong "?d > 14"; right "?d gt 14"; why "angle brackets are only the sequence glyph".
err5 wrong "minh manage zeta @2024-03.."; right "minh manage zeta @2024-03..."; why "the sentence period is still required after an open interval — three dots is legal: .. belongs to the time, . ends the sentence".

// ── self-check before sending ────────────────────────────────
#check steps <c1 c2 c3 c4 c5 c6>.
c1 gloss "every sentence ends with a period; every bracket pair is closed".
c2 gloss "? variables appear only inside { }".
c3 gloss "one thing = one name, everywhere".
c4 gloss "no source edge dropped, no invented edge added" !must.
c5 gloss "assert with plain sentences; anything uncertain goes in [ ] or { } with conf".
c6 gloss "every ; is followed by a predicate; every qualifier is preceded by an object; no rule is wrapped in extra braces" !must.

A taste of it

an a engineer; age 30; love bao @2020.. %80 ~😨%90.
#e1 a deploy; who dev-3; what api-v2; at 2026-07-15T09:30.
#e2 a outage; in region-a; dur 42m. #e1 cause #e2 %85.
{?s a service; error-rate ?r. ?r gt 5} => {?s rollback true !must}.
#p1 a plan; steps <freeze rollback verify postmortem>; owner dev-3.

One line: an engineer, 30, in love since 2020 at strength 80 — colored by fear at 90. Then a deploy, the outage it caused, a mandatory rollback rule, and an ordered recovery plan. Facts, time, emotion, causation, law, and sequence — all edges.
