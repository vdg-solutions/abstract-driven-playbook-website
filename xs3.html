XS3

The first agent-centric language.

Every language you know was designed for humans or for compilers. XS3 is designed for the third audience: LLM agents talking to LLM agents. One sentence is one graph edge. Facts, time, emotion, rules, and law — all first-class, all a few bytes.

Compression without sacrifice

"Summarize this document" always means "quietly lose things". XS3 is not summarization — it is re-encoding. What gets deleted is only what carries no meaning: filler words, repeated subjects, whole clauses that existed just to say when or how much or how strongly forbidden. Those become suffixes that cost a few bytes:

an a engineer; age 30; love bao @2020.. %80 ~😨%90.

One line: an engineer, thirty, in love since 2020 and still, at strength 80 — shadowed by a fear of intensity 90. The prose version of that sentence is three times longer, and no machine can verify a paraphrase of it. The XS3 version is checkable: two documents mean the same thing exactly when they normalize to the same edge set. Fidelity stops being a feeling and becomes a comparison.

Compression you can audit: nothing lost — and "nothing" is machine-checkable.
